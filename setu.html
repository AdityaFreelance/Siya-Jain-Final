We designed a platform that offers curated resources, college-specific requirements, and feedback tools to help aspiring art/design students confidently build portfolios that showcase their skills and creativity.

My Role

As the UI and Research Lead for Setu, I was involved from initial research to final execution. I played a key role in uncovering user needs and translating those insights into clear, actionable design directions.

Here’s a quick summary, if you are in a hurry!

The Problem

Many aspiring design and art students struggle with portfolio creation due to a lack of mentorship, guidance, and structured support, leaving them feeling overwhelmed, isolated, and unprepared to meet college expectations.

The Solution

A platform that supports aspiring art and design students in portfolio creation, enabling them to navigate college requirements with confidence and showcase their talents effectively.

Context

Imagine having all your creative work in one place, but struggling to make it look cohesive and professional.

That was me, lost, undervaluing my projects, and unsure how to present myself.

Without proper guidance, it’s easy for talent to go unnoticed, opportunities to slip by, and your hard work to feel scattered.

Many creatives face the same challenge: having amazing work but no clear way to showcase it effectively.

To move beyond my personal experience, I conducted research.

Main Insights

From our surveys and interviews, we identified key insights, recurring patterns, and quotes that shaped our understanding of the problem and informed our initial approach.

We even chatted with portfolio reviewers to really get a sense of the problem.

To gain a clear view of the problem space, we spoke with portfolio reviewers at SCAD to understand the common issues and hurdles students encounter during portfolio submissions.

  • Lack of cohesiveness

  • Unclear submissions

  • Unfocused work

  • Misread requirements

Key Takeaway

Key struggles for aspiring art students include lack of guidance, managing time, meeting university requirements, organizing work, and finding inspiration.

Learning from the Landscape

We analyzed direct and indirect competitors across four categories (Task and time management tools and learning, mentorship and crowdsourcing platforms) to see what worked, what didn’t, and gather inspiration to shape our own features.

Competitor analysis revealed that leading platforms use gamification, AI-driven guidance, and community interaction to enable personalized learning, portfolio building, mentorship, and active student engagement.

Flows and Wireframes

We drew out our initial ideas taking inspiration from moodboards. These ideas turned out to be our foundation bricks for the rest of our UI development.

homepage

User Profile

  • Profile Overview

  • View personal details (name, art field, progress, entry level)

  • Mentor & Feedback History

  • Keep track of mentor sessions and received feedback

  • Reward & Progress

  • Display collected rewards and task completion

  • Questions to Tailor Experience

  • Art Field Selection

  • Portfolio Experience Level

  • Personal Preferences (e.g., preferred style or focus area)

College & Portfolio Requirements Search

  • University Search

  • List of universities offering art programs (filter by location, major, etc.)

  • Portfolio Criteria

  • Each university page includes portfolio submission requirements and guidelines

  • Display relevant requirements for the user’s chosen major

  • Suggestions

  • Based on the art field, recommend universities that align with the user’s career path

Crowdsourced Gallery

  • Explore Portfolios

  • University-accepted portfolios (filter by major, style, or institution)

  • Exemplary student work (e.g., top-rated, highly praised by mentors)

  • Portfolio Details

  • Clicking on a portfolio opens detailed view (description, artist’s statement, works displayed)

  • Save for Inspiration

  • Option to save or bookmark portfolios for future reference

  • Personal Preferences (e.g., preferred style or focus area)

Mentor Connection

  • Find a Mentor

  • Browse mentors through university partnerships (filter by field or expertise)

  • Profile Details

  • Each mentor has a profile with their qualifications, background, and areas of expertise

  • Request Mentorship

  • Option to request a mentorship session

  • Mentorship details (sessions, feedback history, availability)

Custom Checklist & Deadlines

  • Portfolio Tracker

  • A customized checklist based on the user’s chosen major and art goals

  • Task Deadlines

  • Set deadlines for each checklist item (e.g., complete initial draft, edit, submit)

  • Progress Bar

  • Visual tracker of portfolio progress

  • Gamified Engagement

  • Earn points or rewards for completing tasks or meeting deadlines

Messaging & Instant Feedback

  • Chat Interface

  • Ask questions about portfolio content, layout, or writing

  • Writing assistance for artist statements, personal essays, etc.

    Learnings

    No project is ever easy. Each challenge pushes you, teaches you, and transforms you. Here’s what Setu taught me. Lessons I couldn’t have learned any other way.

    Challenge 1: Letting Go of Personal Bias

    I brought in expectations shaped by my own experience as an international student, assuming what the product should be.

    Learning

    Letting go of personal bias and grounding decisions in research helped me truly understand our audience and approach the problem with fresh eyes.

    Challenge 2: Breaking Out of Visual Comfort Zones

    We defaulted to minimalism and current design trends, even when they didn’t fully resonate with our users.

    Learning

    I learned to take visual risks, embracing bold colors and illustrative styles that better connected with our audience.

    Challenge 3: Wearing Every Hat in a Small Team

    Being part of a small team meant owning every step of the process, which was both overwhelming and revealing.

    Learning

    I discovered the value of leaning into my strengths and trusting my teammates to do the same. It made our collaboration stronger and more effective.
