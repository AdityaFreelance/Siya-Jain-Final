This 10-week project with 30+ designers asked: What should the future of Porsche’s EV interiors feel like? Instead of chasing trends, we built on Porsche’s core values: emotional connection, intuitive performance, and timeless design. With three audiences in mind: first-time buyers, longtime owners, and EV enthusiasts, we created a vision that blends system thinking, storytelling, and design, honoring tradition while imagining what’s next.

My Role

As the Senior Front-End Designer, I led and mentored the front-end design team, creating infotainment and cluster screens across different driving modes to reimagine the future of Porsche’s EV interior experience.

Here’s a quick summary, if you are in a hurry!

The Problem

How can Porsche evolve into the electric era without losing the emotional intensity, precision, and heritage that define its identity? The challenge is balancing decades of analog excellence with the demands of digital innovation, ensuring an EV still feels unmistakably Porsche.

The Solution

We reimagined Porsche’s EV interiors by blending tradition with innovation. Creating intuitive, emotionally resonant interfaces that honor the brand’s legacy while embracing a connected, digital future.

Context

Over 80 years of heritage has made Porsche more than just a car. It’s an icon of emotion, precision, and performance.

But as the world shifts to electric, a question emerges: can an EV truly feel like a Porsche? Can it carry the same intensity, the same soul, without the roar of the engine?

We set out to create an interior experience that answers a call few can articulate but every Porsche driver feels: the need for connection, intuition, and timeless design in a digital age.

A vision not just for driving, but for belonging to a legacy.

To begin, we needed to deeply understand the tension between heritage and innovation and how to merge the two without compromise.

Target Audience

First Time Buyers

Younger professionals entering the Porsche world for the first time. They seek a seamless blend of innovation, luxury, and performance, expecting digital ease without losing the tactile thrill of driving.

Legacy Owners

Loyal Porsche drivers with a deep appreciation for heritage and craftsmanship. They’re open to electric innovation as long as it honors the mechanical soul and dynamic feel they know and trust.

EV owners outside of Porsche

Tech-forward EV drivers seeking more emotion, refinement, and performance. They’re ready to move beyond functional design and embrace a driving experience that feels truly elevated.

Infotainment Guidelines

We outlined the core visual principles and system foundations that guided our interface and experience design. It is rooted in Porsche’s commitment to clarity, precision, and driver focus.

  • Split-Screen Multimodal View

    Allows two essential modules (e.g., navigation + battery info) to be visible simultaneously without overlapping.

  • Context-Aware Homepage

    Automatically displays the most relevant tiles (e.g., charging, navigation) based on real-time driving context.

  • Quick Access Icons

    A simplified home layout with icons for immediate entry to top-level functions. Users expect these fundamentals.

Design sprint

We conducted four distinct design sprints, each focused on a different theme, to spark creativity and explore the possibilities of Porsche’s future EV ecosystem.

Deciding upon final screens

From our explorations, the team converged on one design direction to carry forward as the final concept. It’s an experience that speaks to Porsche’s legacy while resonating with the needs of future EV drivers.

Tech-Lux Minimalism

  • Visual Tone

    High contrast, deep navy and black themes, thin-line icons, Porsche Next type. Think Lucid Motors UI elegance meets heritage mechanics.

  • Interaction Cues

    Subtle motion, tactile feedback. Sliders glide, toggles click, and layered transitions add depth. Feels intuitive, not excessive.

Early Friction

Insights from initial user testing revealed points of friction that guided and shaped our future design iterations.

  • User Hesitation

    Unclear visual hierarchy and cluttered layouts.

  • Misinterpretation

    Terminology confusion (e.g. “eco score,” “internal range”)

  • Mispresses

    High error rate during route planning

  • Bewildered

    Lack of feedback cues

Iterations After User Testing

We analysed key insights given by users from all five rounds of
testing, refining our interfaces into their final designs.

Before

  • The page had limited interactivity, offering few opportunities for users to engage.

  • No option existed for users to change or personalize the playlist.

After

  • Make the page more clickable so users can interact with the page

  • Add a section where users can change the playlist

Before

  • Information was overwhelming and users didn't understand the different modes

  • Users wanted to know more about the changes in the car upon selection

After

  • Simplified information hierarchy made modes easy to understand at a glance

  • Clear visuals highlighted how each mode changed the car’s behavior

Before

  • The energy widget was cluttered and difficult for drivers to interpret quickly

  • Background imagery distracted from the data and added little functional value

After

  • Simplify energy widget to make it more comprehensible for the drivers.

  • Removed image in the energy widget, as it doesn’t serve much purpose.

Before

  • Removed image in the energy widget, as it doesn’t serve much purpose.

After

  • Refined car graphic that clearly showed which section of the vehicle was impacting its range, while maintaining a sleek and visually appealing aesthetic.

Before

  • App widget backgrounds felt disconnected from the overall screen design.

  • Visuals lacked alignment with Porsche’s brand identity and premium aesthetic.

After

  • Change image backgrounds for the app widgets to make it match the overall theme of the screens and match the “Porsche” identity.
